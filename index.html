Chinese paper-cutting, or "Jianzhi," is a traditional folk art that dates back to the 6th century. It involves cutting intricate designs into paper, often using scissors or knives. The art form is deeply rooted in Chinese culture and is commonly used to celebrate festivals, decorate windows, and express good wishes. Common motifs include animals, flowers, and symbolic figures like the Chinese zodiac signs. Chinese paper-cutting is recognized for its delicate craftsmanship and vibrant colors, representing prosperity, happiness, and good fortune. It has been listed as an intangible cultural heritage by UNESCO.
